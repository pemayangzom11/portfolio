Project Overview

The Product

The main goal of this project is to provide users with reliable, fast, and precise identification of plant diseases from uploaded images, followed by actionable treatment plans to ensure their plants return to health swiftly.

Project information

  • Category:website
  • Project Duration: March 2025 - June, 2025

PROBLEM

Plant owners often find it difficult to accurately identify and treat diseases in their houseplants. They need a fast and dependable way to recognize problems and receive effective care advice.

THE GOAL

To develop a user-friendly and reliable website that enables plant owners to quickly upload images of their houseplants, accurately detect diseases, and receive effective treatment advice without encountering technical difficulties.

ROLE

UX/UI Designer. [UI (user interface), UX (user experience)] frontend developer

Responsibilities

Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs, determining information architecture, and responsive design.

User Research: Pain Point

01

Difficulty identifying plant diseases accurately just by symptoms or appearance. .

02

Lack of reliable and accessible guidance for treating houseplant illnesses.

03

Most existing tools are too technical or not user-friendly for everyday plant owners.

Accessibility Considerations

  1. Low-contrast elements like filter buttons and icons were difficult to identify.Increasing the contrast ratio, icon size, and adding labels.
  2. Utilized headings with different fonts and typefaces to establish a clear visual hierarchy, making it easier for users to understand and navigate the content.
  3. TDiagnosis result images and buttons lacked alt text and descriptive labels.Ensuring all diagnosis visuals, treatment icons, and action buttons include meaningful alt text and ARIA labels supports users who rely on screen readers for navigation and enhances the accessibility of the platform.

Going Forward

Impact

Users shared that the app made plant conservation feel like something they could actively contribute to. One quote from peer feedback was that 'the Plant Medic app helps bring caring for plant life and biodiversity to a personal level in a way that’s easy and engaging

What I learned

I learned that even though the problem I was trying to solve was a big one, diligently going through each step of the design process and aligning with specific user needs helped me come up with solutions that were both feasible and useful.
