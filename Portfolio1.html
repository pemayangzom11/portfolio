Project Overview

The Product

BakeBliss which is to provide a fast, seamless, and user-friendly digital platform for ordering bakery items.

Project information

  • Category: Mobile Application
  • Project Duration: March 2025 - June, 2025

PROBLEM

Baking enthusiasts and home cooks often face challenges such as inconsistent recipes, lack of step-by-step guidance, and difficulty in finding trusted baking tips, which can lead to failed results and wasted ingredients.

THE GOAL

The goal of the BakeBliss is to provide a seamless, fast, and user-friendly digital platform that enables users to browse bakery items, select, order, and make payments effortlessly.

ROLE

UX/UI Designer. [UI (user interface), UX (user experience)] frontend developer

Responsibilities

User research, User persona, Problem statement, User journey map, User flow, Storyboard, Low fidelity wireframe, Low fidelity prototypes, High fidelity mockups, High fidelity prototypes, Usability studies

User Research: Pain Point

01

Limited Product Customization Options

Users wanted more flexibility to customize orders (e.g., icing type, flavors, allergy preferences), but the app offered only basic choices.

02

No Order Tracking Feature

After placing an order, users had no way to track the status or progress of their delivery, leading to uncertainty and repeated inquiries.

03

Limited Payment Options

Some users were frustrated by the lack of diverse payment methods, especially those who preferred digital wallets or cash-on-delivery.

Usability Study

Tasks tested in usability study included:
1. Time on task (from item selection to checkout completion)
2. view Drop-off rate during payment

Accessibility Considerations

  1. High-Quality Images with Alt Text: All product photos are displayed in high resolution and include descriptive alt text, allowing visually impaired users to understand what each item looks like via screen readers.
  2. Clear, Accessible Order Tracking Updates: Real-time order tracking is provided in both visual and text formats, ensuring that users with cognitive or visual impairments can stay informed about their delivery status.
  3. Simple and Inclusive Payment Options: The checkout process includes multiple accessible payment methods (e.g., digital wallets, mobile payments, and simplified one-click checkout), reducing friction for users with mobility or attention challenges.
